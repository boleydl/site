General Information
This course introduces the basic numerical techniques to solve mathematical problems on a digital computer. Algorithms for several common problems encountered in computer science, mathematics, science and engineering are introduced. The pitfalls and errors that can arise when solving mathematical problems with methods taking finite time and in finite precision arithmetic are discussed, and measures to predict when such pitfalls are encountered will be introduced.

Computer Platform

Students will be expected to implement several of the algorithms on a digital computer using python with a limited set of packages (e.g., numpy, matplotlib, pdb, plus a few functions listed explicitly from other packages). In the cases where another equivalent interactive programming environment is allowed, you will be responsible for implementing the equivalent features and may find only limited help from the instructional staff. Students should be familiar with basic programming techniques. Students should also be acquainted with the basic concepts of the more elementary mathematical and numerical methods (e.g. solving simple linear equations, root-finding, computing averages, using derivatives to find the minimum of a scalar function, etc.) though some of this will be reviewed during the course.

Some assignments may be assigned to be done by pairs of students; such items should be handed in as a single item listing the names of all participants. Late Homework Submissions

Homeworks will be accepted until answers have been posted or discussed in class or up to three working days after the due date (whichever occurs first). For regular homeworks, the late penalty will grow nonlinearly with the delay: 1% if within 12 hours ( only once during the semester), 2% if within 24 hours, 10% up to 2 days, 20% if more. The penalty will be doubled for items with due dates within 1 day of the posting date, and anything due in the last week of class. The score posted for any assignment might not include any late penalty, which may be imposed and recorded later as a separate item. If unable to complete a quiz during class, you should hand in what you can in class, and hand in your more complete answer by 11:59pm the same day. Quizzes handed during class will be scored based on a good faith effort. Quizzes handed in later will be scored on correctness, with little room for partial credit. Late pop quizzes will receive only half credit. We suggest you photograph your in-class quiz with your cell phone before handing it in. Answers could be posted any time after the due date/time without advance notice. Whether or not a per-assignment penalty is applied or waived based on a reasonable and unanticipated excuse, the final semester accumulated score will be reduced by 1 point for each item handed in late.
